Battery research advisory board

THE ADVISORY BOARD

Bob Galyen (Chair)

Galyen Energy / NAATBatt International

Bob Galyen is a globally recognized battery expert with nearly 50 years of experience in the automotive and energy storage industries. He served in leadership roles at GM, Delphi, Magna, and as CTO of CATL, the world’s largest EV battery manufacturer. Today he leads Galyen Energy, serves on multiple corporate and advisory boards, and is CTO of NAATBatt International.

Dr. James Trevey

Forge Nano / NAATBatt

Dr. James Trevey is CTO of Forge Nano and an expert in lithium-ion batteries, materials science, and technology scaling. He is President-Elect of NAATBatt, Founder and Vice President of the Military Power Sources Consortium, and Industry Lead for Li-Bridge, advancing domestic energy security through collaboration across industry and government.

Dr. John Warner

American Battery Solutions / Warner Energy Consulting LLC

Dr. John Warner is Chief Customer Officer at American Battery Solutions and President of Warner Energy Consulting LLC. With over 30 years in the automotive and battery industries, he has held executive roles at EnerDel, XALT Energy, Magna Steyr, Boston-Power, and General Motors. He is a Chairman Emeritus of NAATBatt International, serves on SAE battery standards committees, and is the author of two widely used books on lithium-ion batteries.

Shawn Murphy

Titan Advanced Energy Solutions

Shawn Murphy is Co-founder, CEO, and CTO of Titan Advanced Energy Solutions. An entrepreneur and technologist with more than 25 years of experience, he has founded multiple startups and led innovation programs in aerospace, defense, semiconductors, oil and gas, and energy systems. Previously, he directed Earth and Space Science at Draper Laboratory and launched Shell TechWorks, a technology commercialization center for Royal Dutch Shell.

Dr. Alec Falzone

Honeywell

Dr. Alec Falzone is Growth Leader for Battery Manufacturing at Honeywell. He has built his career in the lithium-ion battery industry across both technical and commercial roles, applying advanced measurement, digitization, and process control solutions to battery manufacturing. He holds a Ph.D. in Inorganic Chemistry from North Carolina State University.

Lucas (Luke) Wells

BI by UL Solutions / Energy Wells

Luke Wells is a Scientist at BI by UL Solutions in Aachen, Germany, with more than 15 years of experience in engineering, chemical engineering, and materials science. He leads projects on next-generation lithium-ion cell manufacturing, failure analysis, and lab growth strategy. Luke is also founder of Energy Wells, a consultancy focused on scaling lithium-ion cell production and optimizing manufacturing efficiency.

Michael Malley

AMP Center

Michael Malley serves as Manager of Strategic Research Partnerships at the AMP Center within the Alabama Transportation Institute. He leads the implementation of both the Battery Research Center and Power Research Center and has been deeply involved in AMP’s strategic mission spanning use-inspired research, economic development, and workforce development. Prior to his current role, Malley managed UA’s State Apprenticeship Extension Grant and Alabama Transfers initiative, contributing to workforce and education policy initiatives.

Workforce development

WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT STEERING COMMITTEE

Dr. Joshua Bittle

The University of Alabama

Dr. Joshua Bittle is an Associate Professor at the Lee J. Styslinger Jr. College of Engineering and Associate Director of the Center for Advanced Vehicle Technologies (CAVT), specializing in engineering and applied research. His work drives innovation in advanced vehicle technologies and helps connect academic research with industry needs.

Kevin Taylor

AIDT

Kevin Taylor is Deputy Director of AIDT, Alabama's primary workforce development agency supporting industrial recruitment and training. He works to align workforce programs with the needs of advanced manufacturing and the growing EV and battery ecosystem.

Barry May

Alabama Community College System

Barry May serves as Chief Workforce Development Officer with the Alabama Community College System, helping lead workforce education and training initiatives across the state. His work focuses on developing talent pipelines to support high-demand technical industries.

Dana Bubonovich

Volta Foundation

Dana Bubonovich is Vice President of Business Opertation with the Volta Foundation, a global nonprofit advancing battery innovation and industry collaboration. She works to connect leaders across the battery ecosystem and support knowledge sharing and workforce development.

Ajay Gnanasekaran

Argonne National Laboratory

Ajay Gnanasekaran is Senior Program Director of the Battery Workforce Challenge at Argonne National Laboratory, a DOE-sponsored program building a domestic workforce for battery manufacturing and engineering. He brings deep expertise in battery technology, manufacturing, and workforce strategy to support the growing demands of the US EV industry.

Renu Dalal

Workforce Program Manager, AMP Center

Renu Dalal is the Workforce Program Manager at the AMP Center, where she leads efforts to develop talent pipelines for the evolving energy and mobility sectors. She works with industry, education, and workforce partners to design training programs that align with the needs of battery manufacturing, electrification, and advanced power systems.

Power research lab

POWER RESEARCH LAB STEERING COMMITTEE

LaQuisha Buggs

Alabama Power

LaQuisha Buggs serves as Energy Services Manager at Alabama Power Company, where she contributes to grid modernization and customer-focused energy solutions. Her work supports the integration of new technologies and infrastructure to meet evolving power demands across the state.

Dr. Ben Gully

SPOC Energy

Dr. Ben Gully is Chief Technologist at SPOC Grid Inverter Technologies, where he leads work on advanced energy storage systems, power electronics, and grid-integrated solutions. With over 15 years of experience in maritime, stationary, and industrial applications, his expertise spans battery system design, hybrid power architectures, and electrification infrastructure.

Nick Warner

ESRG

Nick Warner is Principal and Co-Founder of Energy Safety Response Group (ESRG), specializing in battery safety, hazard mitigation, and emergency response training. He works closely with industry and first responders to improve safety protocols across the energy storage ecosystem.

Brian Engle

Amphenol

Brian Engle is Director of Business Development with Amphenol, a global leader in interconnect and sensor technologies supporting advanced manufacturing and electrification. He brings expertise in high-performance connectivity solutions critical to EVs and energy systems.

Clayton Gibbons

SPOC Energy

Clayton Gibbons is Head of Power Systems with SPOC Energy where he works on the development and deployment of advanced power conversion technologies. His efforts support grid resiliency and scalable infrastructure for electrification.

Fernanda Foertter

UA High Performance Computing and Data Center

Fernanda Foertter is Executive Director of The University of Alabama's High Performance Computing and Data Center, where she is leading the development of a new 40,000-square-foot facility set to open in 2026. Her work connects advanced computing, AI, and data science with real-world research and industry applications, supporting innovation and workforce development across Alabama.

Jason Powers

eVerged

Jason Powers is Senior Vice President of Sales at eVerged, where he leads initiatives in EV charging innovation and deployment. He focuses on advancing autonomous and portable charging solutions for next-generation mobility.

Michael Malley

AMP Center

Michael Malley serves as Director of Research Programs at the AMP Center, where he leads the development and execution of industry-aligned research initiatives. He works closely with consortium partners to advance projects in battery technology, power systems, and grid innovation, helping bridge academic research with real-world application.
