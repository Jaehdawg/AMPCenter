Battery, power, consumer, workforce, and consortium engagement.

The AMP Center’s work spans research, commercialization, and workforce development.

BUSINESS INTELLIGENCE and CONSUMER RESEARCH.

Electrification success depends on consumer behavior, infrastructure readiness, and market dynamics.

AMP provides business intelligence and consumer research to help partners anticipate demand, understand adoption barriers, and guide deployment decisions. Powered by EVOLVE, AMP’s advanced analytics and modeling tool, these insights integrate consumer behavior data, charging usage patterns, and regional adoption trends to support infrastructure planning, strategic investment, and market deployment.

WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT.

The transition to electrified mobility and advanced energy systems requires a highly skilled workforce prepared to operate, maintain, and scale next-generation technologies.

AMP aligns workforce training with real-world manufacturing environments and evolving power system requirements to ensure talent is ready on day one. Through hands-on learning and industry-informed curriculum, AMP helps build a workforce capable of supporting battery production, electrification infrastructure, and advanced energy operations.

INDUSTRY COLLABORATION and CONSORTIUM ENGAGEMENT.

AMP serves as a collaborative platform where industry, academia, and government partners work together to solve real-world challenges, validate technologies, and accelerate commercialization.

Through the AMP Industry Consortium — a growing network of 45+ member organizations spanning the mobility, battery, energy, and manufacturing sectors — partners engage directly with research initiatives, workforce development efforts, and strategic planning shaping the future of mobility and power.
