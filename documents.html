BATTERY INNOVATION and  
PILOT LINE ACCESS.
AMP’s Battery Research Center supports the full lifecycle of battery 
development — from material evaluation and cell design to manu -
facturing scale-up and commercialization readiness. By combining 
applied research with pilot-scale production capabilities, AMP 
helps industry partners reduce technical risk, validate processes, 
and accelerate time to market. Located within the Battery Research 
Center, these capabilities provide a unique environment to validate 
technologies, test materials, and scale manufacturing processes.
Capabilities include:
 » Pouch cell pilot line for process validation and scale-up
 » Manufacturing readiness and production workflow optimization
 » Cell testing, performance evaluation, & material characterization
 » Technology validation and collaborative research
 » Industry consulting and technical partnerships
These capabilities support commercialization, reduce scale-up risk, 
and strengthen the domestic battery supply chain.
POWER and GRID  
RESILIENCE RESEARCH.
As electrification accelerates and energy demand grows, resilient 
and adaptive power systems are essential. AMP’s power research 
lab enables utilities, manufacturers, and technology providers to 
model, test, and validate solutions that improve reliability, optimize 
energy use, and support emerging high-load applications.
Through advanced simulation and real-world testing, partners can 
evaluate next-generation energy systems and infrastructure in a 
controlled, collaborative environment.
Capabilities include:
 » Grid resiliency and outage recovery scenarios
 » Demand shifting and load management strategies
 » Grid-independent charging approaches
 » Integration of distributed energy resources
 » Resilience planning for data centers and high-load infrastructure
A collaborative demonstration space is available for industry testing 
and validation.
The Alabama Power & Mobility (AMP) Center brings together 
industry, researchers, and workforce partners to accelerate 
innovation in battery manufacturing, power and grid resilience, 
charging infrastructure, and electrification. With more than 50 
industry and strategic partners engaged through its consortium, 
the AMP Center fosters collaboration that connects research,  
commercialization, and workforce development.
Our mission is to drive research, workforce development, and 
economic growth in sustainable mobility and power systems.

BATTERY RESEARCH CENTER
PILOT MANUFACTURING.
AMP Center’s pouch cell pilot line supports validation of battery 
production processes across the core stages of lithium-ion  
battery manufacturing.
Process capabilities include:
 » Material characterization
 » Slurry mixing and electrode formulation
 » Electrode coating and drying
 » Electrode calendaring
 » Electrode slitting and preparation
 » Coin cell assembly and pouch cell fabrication
 » Cell formation and conditioning
This pilot-scale manufacturing environment allows partners to 
refine production workflows, evaluate materials, and demonstrate 
manufacturability prior to commercial-scale deployment. 
MATERIALS CHARACTERIZATION.
AMP Center’s prototyping philosophy begins with rigorous material 
characterization and quality control. The facility provides advanced 
analytical capabilities to evaluate raw materials and battery  
components according to the standards expected by current  
and future customers.
Capabilities include:
 » BET specific surface area analysis
 » Particle size analysis
 » True, tap, and bulk density measurements
 » Inductively coupled plasma (ICP) and gas 
chromatography analysis
 » Differential scanning calorimetry (DSC) and 
thermogravimetric analysis (TGA)
 » Scanning electron microscopy (SEM) with energy-dispersive 
X-ray spectroscopy (EDS)
 » Argon ion cross-sectioning and polishing
 » Plasma FIB milling for site-specific cross-sectioning and 
final surface preparation
BATTERY INNOVATION, TESTING & PILOT MANUFACTURING
The AMP Battery Research Center provides an integrated environment for 
battery development, testing, research, and pilot-scale manufacturing. 
The facility supports the transition of battery technologies from laboratory 
research to manufacturing readiness by enabling partners to validate  
materials, optimize electrode and cell designs, refine production processes, 
and evaluate cell performance under real-world operating conditions.
Through pilot manufacturing capabilities and advanced electrochemical 
testing infrastructure, the Battery Research Center helps industry partners 
reduce technical risk, improve cell performance, and accelerate the  
commercialization of next-generation battery technologies.

SMART MANUFACTURING and 
AUTOMATION.
The facility incorporates advanced automation technologies, 
 including Honeywell’s AI-powered Battery Manufacturing  
Excellence Platform (Battery MXP). This platform helps optimize  
manufacturing operations, improve cell yields, and accelerate  
battery production facility startup timelines.
By reducing scrap and improving process efficiency, these automa-
tion tools lower the financial barrier to entry for cell prototyping 
and pilot-scale development.
Through the integration of pilot manufacturing, advanced testing 
infrastructure, and manufacturing automation tools, the AMP 
Battery Research Center provides industry with a collaborative 
environment to develop, validate, and scale next-generation  
battery technologies. 
FAILURE ANALYSIS.
The facility incorporates advanced analytical equipment to support 
root-cause analysis of battery failures and performance issues. AMP 
investigates both internally developed and externally tested cells, 
as well as failure mechanisms at the module and pack level.
Capabilities include:
 » Computed tomography (CT)
 » Transmission electron microscopy (TEM)
 » Atom probe tomography (APT)
BATTERY TESTING and  
VALIDATION.
The Battery Research Center includes advanced electrochemical 
testing infrastructure used to evaluate battery performance,  
durability, and degradation mechanisms.
Testing capabilities include:
 » Cycle life, durability, and aging testing
 » Capacity and rate performance evaluation
 » Formation testing for pouch cells
 » High-current cell testing up to 250A
 » Rapid electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) analysis
 » Ultra-high precision coulomb counting for advanced 
cell characterization
Testing is conducted in temperature-controlled environmental 
chambers ranging from -40°C to 180°C, enabling evaluation across 
a wide range of operating conditions. 
Battery testing platforms include:
 » Digatron Cell Testers: circuits ranging from 10A to 250A with
Safion rapid EIS capability
 » Safion pouch cell formation circuits with rapid EIS capabilities
 » Neware Cell Testers: circuits ranging from 10 mA to 50A
 » Novonix Ultra-High Precision Cell Testers: ultra-high precision
coulomb counting for detailed electrochemical characterization

INTEGRATED ENERGY  
SYSTEMS INNOVATION.
By combining grid simulation, electrification research, and advanced 
communications infrastructure, the AMP Power Research Lab provides a 
comprehensive platform to evaluate how next-generation energy systems 
perform in real-world conditions.
From resilient grid operations to connected charging infrastructure and 
intelligent energy management, the lab enables partners to test, validate, 
and deploy solutions that support large-scale electrification and future 
energy demands. Through this integrated approach, AMP helps industry 
and utilities reduce deployment risk, improve system performance, and 
accelerate the development of reliable, scalable energy infrastructure.
POWER RESEARCH LAB AT A GLANCE
Full-Scale Test Environment
~10,000 sq ft indoor lab with outdoor equipment pad
Utility Service
2,000 A, 480 V, 3-phase primary service
DC Power Infrastructure
1,100 VDC SPOC DC Power Grid system
Power Conversion Capacity
2 MW AC inverter capacity (2 × 1 MW)
1 MW DC-DC conversion capacity (2 × 500 kW)
On-Site Generation
1.25 MW diesel generator (microgrid system)
Energy Storage (BESS)
250 kWh current capacity (Smartville 360)
Expandable to ~1.5 MWh total capacity
Renewable Energy
30 kW rooftop solar PV array
EV Charging Infrastructure
12 charging stations (DC fast charge + Level 2)
Connected Systems
Private 5G ORAN network for real-time communication 
and control
Controls & Monitoring
Integrated PLC, SCADA, HMI, and advanced control systems
SMART INFRASTRUCTURE and  
CONNECTED ENERGY SYSTEMS. 
The AMP Power Research Lab integrates advanced communications 
technologies with power systems to support the next generation of 
connected intelligent energy infrastructure. As electrification and  
digitalization converge, reliable, secure, and high-performance  
connectivity becomes essential to enabling real-time control,  
automation, and optimization of energy systems.
Through partnerships with industry leaders, AMP provides a secure, 
real-world environment to test how communications networks, power 
systems, and connected devices operate together at scale.
5G ORAN INNOVATION TESTBED
AMP operates a private 5G ORAN (Open Radio Access Network) testbed 
that enables secure, real-time communication across connected 
energy and mobility systems. This platform supports integration of 
grid infrastructure, EV charging, and connected devices—providing the 
foundation for advanced applications such as autonomous charging, 
smart grid management, and industrial IoT .
Key capabilities include:
 » Low-latency, secure connectivity for energy systems 
 » Real-time monitoring and control of grid and charging infrastructure 
 » Support for autonomous and mobile charging applications 
 » Integration of industrial IoT and connected devices 
The 5G ORAN environment enables partners to validate connected 
technologies in a scalable, real-world setting.
